2018 Retrospective - My first year in the web

7min read

December 31, 2018

This is my first year on the web, and I want to make a sort of retrospective to summarize what went good and what went bad in my 2018 and what I plan for the future.

I still have to get used to blog writing, this post will be confusonary, you have been warned.

How it started - The vision

At the beginning of 2018 I opened this blog, withouth really knowing what to write in it. I just wanted a personal place on the web to put some info (email, Twitter, Github/Gitlab profiles) where to talk about what I did during my time at University.

I did it primarly because I was searching for a job and, without experience, I could only show my personal projects, so I used this website as a “portfolio”.

But then time passed and I wanted something different from it: I wanted a place where other people could find me and know me better.

Today I look at this website and I honestly don’t like it, I don’t feel like it’s representative of my personality. I’ll probably reorganize the content and the style in the next year and I’ll write more about myself. This website is terribly anonymous at the moment and I want to improve it.

After my first year I can say that I have a better idea of the direction that I want to take with it.

  • I moved to London to work as a Full Stack Web Developer for a growing startup
  • I worked for 10 months, it was a really exciting experience, I learned lots of things about unit testing, e2e testing, Typescript, Angular4/5/6, Docker, Fluent
  • Dino Path

January - Searching a job

January: I started searching for jobs, I wanted to work abroad (I don’t feel like working in Italy, unfortunately) so I searched for jobs in UK and Germany.

February - I found a job + moving to UK

February: I found a job as a fullstack web developer with a growing startup, so I moved there.

It was my first job and my first experience with a foreign country (apart from occasional trips). I was excited and worried at the same time, and this stressing situation gave me a lot of energies, probably it’s human nature: your body release more energy if you are under pressure.

March - My first month as a software developer

Working with an agile team was a new thing for me: I was only used to collaborate with others with Github/Gitlab issue tracking system and with the common communication systems used by open source projects (IRC, Gitter, …).

The team with which I worked used an Agile/Scrum environment. I remember exactly being really shy during the first days: I was scared of doing a stand-up daily scrum, I was worried about my spoken English.

April - New friends + EGX Rezzed

During this time I found an Italian online community of game developers, Gameloop.it.

Many of them were already living in London and they were attending the EGX Rezzed so we met in person at this nice event. It was really nice to see a gaming industry event in UK: I never saw something similar in Italy. Videogames are taken seriously in UK and this event is pretty representative. Big spaces, excellent organization, lots of videogames, developers and talks.

But I was really glad to meet some member of Gameloop: Matteo (aka Corrado), Antonio, Rudi. They told me about a nice videogames event in Italy, called Svilupparty and since I’m all about videogames, I decided to book a flight to go there in the next month.

May - Gameloop, Svilupparty and Harvard CS50 gamedev course

I flied back to Italy, in Bologna, where I attended the Svilupparty together with my friend Pasquale Salvati. In Bologna we met other Gameloop community members, I met personally Gianluca (aka theGiallo), Angelo (aka Encelo), Manuele (aka ManHunter), Giulio and many others (I’m probably missing someone in this list).

It was really nice knowing each other, talking about videogames and drinking at the “baretto” in the beautiful Bologna. I’m looking forward to going back in 2019.

Rudi told me about Harvard’s CS50 gamedev course, an online free course about game programming with Lua, Love2D and Unity3D. I already wanted to dive into game development as a side project, so it was a perfect coincidence.

I decided to take this course and also coordinate a group of other fellow Gameloop members that wanted to take this course. I organized deadlines, I made some live coding on Twitch, and curated a thread on the Gameloop forum.

Now (December 2018) we just finished the course and we published our assignments (with all the source code) on this Github repository: https://github.com/GameLoop-it/cs50_course_materials

June - Crystal Bit project

Crystal Bit is a project (for Italian-speaking audience) that I started to keep doing the things that I loved doing during University: talking about game development, game design and open source software.

When I started this project I had a schedule in mind: release a new video every 2 weeks. It was not easy because I had a full time job and I didn’t have experience with video-making. YouTube, video editing, tutorials, etc. I didn’t have any experience at all.

In 7 months I created 13 video tutorials for the Godot Game Engine (v3.0) with 200 subscribers and 30’000 minutes watched.

They are not impressive numbers, but I’m really proud of what I achieved. Especially considering that I didn’t feel suited at all when I started; I was actually scared of receiving negative feedback and instead all the comments and the people that got in touch with me were super friendly! Thanks everyone!

I’ll write more about Crystal Bit in the next weeks, I’m planning about opening a dedicated website, so that I can have Italian content on it and keep this blog in English and open for a wider audience.

July, August, September - Working hard

During these months I had a pretty busy schedule: deadline with my full-time job, creating videos for Crystal Bit YT channel and keep studying gamedev with the CS50 Harvard’s course.

Having little free time forces you to prioritize and optimize your time: something that we should do always, but it’s not easy to enforce because humans are inherently lazy.

October - Quitting my first job

November - Coming back to Italy

December - Devember and implementing Triple Triad with Godot

What went well (in 2018)

Actually 2018 was a really good year.

  • terribly improved my English
  • had my first full time job as a software developer
  • improved my confidence
  • started loving taken flights and trains
  • started a YouTube channel
  • managed to keep my publish schedule (2 videos per month) while working fulltime on a startup releasing a world-wide web application
  • streamd game programming on Twitch with an Italian community (gameloop)
  • understood the importance of prioritizing when you have scarce time resources
  • experienced how it is living in a big city like London
  • met a lot of new people, new cultures
  • made new friends (gameloop pic)
  • I survived a crazy trekking day on a snowy mountain

What could be improved

What I’ll commit to improve in 2019

There is a time in your life when you feel that a change is needed. Decisions are not easy to take, but sometimes taking a leap can be a life changing decision.

On January I was really depressed and unmotivated, even if I was living with my family, in a lovely Italian village sorrounded by the mountains and I was just doing what I loved: playing guitar, contributing to open source projects (FreeCodeCamp, GDQuest,…), building my portfolio.

I packed the most essential things in a small bag and I left that wonderful place, in search for a job and a new place.
